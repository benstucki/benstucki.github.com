If you're at all interested in the future of Rich Internet Applications, it's time you heard about the Reflex project. Reflex is, in short, a light-weight toolkit for developing application in MXML or AS3 WITHOUT Flex. That's right - no Flex.

Tyler Wright and I were on hand at 360|MAX last week to unveil plans for the framework on Adobe's own turf. It's still very early and there's not a lot of code to work with yet, but we were able to answer some basic questions.

Is Reflex pure AS3 or built on top of the Flex framework SDK?
Reflex is written in both MXML and AS3, but DOES NOT use the Flex framework (think tiny swfs)
Are Reflex components based on composition or inheritance?
Reflex favors composition over inheritance almost religiously
What is the estimated swf size of a Reflex application?
Reflex is pay-as-you-go, so size will vary depending on how many features you use. Our goal is to include Buttons, ScrollBars, Containers and Lists at around 40 - 80 kb all together.
Does Reflex use Flash Player 10
Yes. Reflex will not work in FP9 or below.
Do you plan to compete with Flex?
Yes, we plan to compete with Flex
When?!
Expect a preview release in December and a Beta early next year.
